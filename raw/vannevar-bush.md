# Vannevar Bush

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vannevar_Bush

Vannevar Bush (1890-1974) was an American engineer, inventor, and science
administrator. He led the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development
during World War II, coordinating wartime research including the Manhattan Project.

Bush published "As We May Think" in The Atlantic in July 1945. The essay
described the Memex and argued that scientific knowledge was outpacing human
ability to navigate it. He proposed mechanical aids for thought.

His 1945 report "Science, The Endless Frontier" laid the groundwork for the
National Science Foundation, established in 1950. Bush mentored generations of
researchers at MIT, including Claude Shannon and Frederick Terman.

He held over 49 patents. He built the Differential Analyzer, an early analog
computer, in 1931. His writings continue to influence personal knowledge
management, hypertext design, and information retrieval.
