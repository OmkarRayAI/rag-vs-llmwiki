# Memex

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memex

The Memex is a hypothetical electromechanical device described by Vannevar Bush
in his 1945 essay "As We May Think", published in The Atlantic. The Memex stores
an individual's books, records, and communications, and mechanizes them so they
may be consulted with exceeding speed and flexibility.

Bush envisioned associative trails, where users link related items. This concept
prefigures the hyperlink and is widely cited as an inspiration for hypertext,
the World Wide Web, and personal knowledge management systems.

Ted Nelson coined the term "hypertext" in 1963, partly inspired by Bush. Doug
Engelbart's NLS demonstration in 1968 showed early implementations of associative
linking. Tim Berners-Lee created the World Wide Web in 1989, citing Bush as
intellectual ancestor.

The Memex was never built. Its key unresolved problem: who maintains the trails.
Bush assumed the user would. Modern proposals route this maintenance through
LLMs.
