# Personal Knowledge Management

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_knowledge_management

Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) is a process of collecting, classifying,
storing, searching, retrieving, and sharing knowledge in one's daily activities.
The term was popularized by Jason Frand and Carol Hixon at UCLA in the late 1990s.

PKM systems span paper journals, hypertext outliners, wikis, and modern
note-taking apps. Tiago Forte's "Building a Second Brain", published in 2022,
codified a four-step framework: Capture, Organize, Distill, Express (CODE).

Tools include Notion, Roam Research, Obsidian, Logseq, Evernote, and Apple Notes.
Each trades off structure, linking, plain-text portability, and synchronization.

The shift toward LLM-maintained PKM proposes that tedious bookkeeping —
linking, summarizing, deduplicating, indexing — can be delegated to agents,
freeing the human to read and think.
