# Zettelkasten

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zettelkasten

The Zettelkasten (German for "slip-box") is a personal note-taking method
developed by sociologist Niklas Luhmann. Luhmann produced over 90,000 notes
across his career and credited the system for his prolific output, including
70 books and 400 scholarly articles.

Each note in a Zettelkasten contains a single atomic idea. Notes carry unique
identifiers and reference other notes by ID. Connections form an emergent
network rather than a fixed hierarchy.

Sönke Ahrens popularized the method in his 2017 book "How to Take Smart Notes".
Modern digital implementations include Roam Research, Obsidian, Logseq,
and Org-roam.

Core principles: atomicity (one idea per note), unique identifiers, dense
linking, fleeting versus permanent notes, and progressive summarization.
The Zettelkasten is often cited as a precursor to LLM-maintained knowledge
graphs.
