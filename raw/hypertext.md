# Hypertext

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypertext

Hypertext is text displayed on a computer or other electronic device with
references, called hyperlinks, to other text the reader can immediately access.
Ted Nelson coined the term in 1963 and elaborated it in his 1965 paper "A File
Structure for the Complex, the Changing, and the Indeterminate".

Nelson's Project Xanadu, started in 1960, attempted a comprehensive hypertext
system with bidirectional links and version control. Xanadu was never fully
released. Doug Engelbart's NLS demonstrated working hypertext in 1968.

Tim Berners-Lee combined hypertext with internet protocols to create the World
Wide Web in 1989. HTML, the HyperText Markup Language, became the dominant
hypertext format.

Wiki software, invented by Ward Cunningham in 1995 as WikiWikiWeb, made
hypertext editable by readers. Wikis influenced personal knowledge management
tools that adopt `[[wiki-link]]` syntax, including Obsidian, Roam, and Logseq.
