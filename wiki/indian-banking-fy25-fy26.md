# Indian Banking, FY25 → FY26

Themes that are visible from the BCG industry-snapshot pages alone. Each
claim cites the period(s) where it shows up; per-bank detail lives in
the PDFs.

## 1. Volume growth steady, profit growth halved

Deposits: +10–12% YoY across all six periods. Advances: +11–13% YoY.
Steady. PAT growth, however, decelerated sharply across FY26: +20% YoY
in H1 FY25 and 9M FY25, +16% in FY25, then **+3% Q1 FY26, +3% H1 FY26,
+6% 9M FY26**. The volume engine kept running; the price engine slowed.

## 2. NIM compression is the proximate cause

NII YoY collapsed from +8–10% in FY25 to +2–3% across FY26. The headline
NIM print fell from 3.1% (FY25) to 3.0% (H1 FY26 / 9M FY26), -21 to -27
bps YoY in every FY26 period. Repricing on the deposit side and a
softer asset-yield environment is the standard read; the decks
themselves quantify it but per-bank attribution is in the page-3+
material not yet ingested.

## 3. Non-interest income carried FY26 results

Non-interest income grew **+44% YoY in Q1 FY26**, then +23% (H1 FY26)
and +22% (9M FY26), versus +14–18% in FY25. Without that, PAT growth in
FY26 would have been negative or near zero. This is a quality-of-
earnings flag for any deeper analysis: how much of FY26 PAT survival is
treasury / fee-income vs. core?

## 4. CASA continues to bleed

CASA ratio fell from 37.1% (H1 FY25) to 35.9% (9M FY26). YoY deltas were
sharply negative for FY25 (-130 to -198 bps) and moderating but still
negative through FY26 (-93 to -169 bps). Translation: customers moving
balances from current/savings to term deposits, raising the cost of
funds — consistent with the NIM compression in (2).

## 5. GNPA still improving

GNPA fell every period: 2.6% → 2.4% → 2.2% → 2.2% → 2.1% → 1.9%, with
YoY improvements of -47 to -72 bps. Asset quality is the bright spot
through both years.

## 6. MSME outpaces retail

MSME advances grew faster than retail in every period (+14–18% YoY vs.
+12–15% retail). Both above headline advances growth. Mix shift toward
MSME is consistent across the series.

## 7. ROA softened despite better NPA

ROA was 1.4% in FY25 interim periods, then 1.3% from FY25 full-year
onward. YoY went from +4 to +9 bps (positive) in FY25 to -6 to -10 bps
(negative) in FY26. Lower NIM bit harder than improving GNPA helped.

## 8. The ROA tree by bank category, 9M FY26

Source: parsed pages 12–13 of the 9M FY26 deck
(`raw/parsed/banking-sector-roundup-9mfy26.parsed.md`). All figures as
% of average assets.

|                       | Industry | PSU  | Pvt-New | Pvt-Old | SFB  |
|-----------------------|---------:|-----:|--------:|--------:|-----:|
| Net Interest Margin   |     3.0% | 2.5% |    3.6% |    3.3% | 6.1% |
| Fee + Other Income    |     1.3% | 1.1% |    1.6% |    1.3% | 2.1% |
| Operating Expenses    |     2.1% | 1.8% |    2.4% |    2.5% | 5.2% |
| Pre-Provision Profit  |     2.2% | 1.8% |    2.9% |    2.1% | 2.9% |
| Credit Costs          |     0.5% | 0.3% |    0.7% |    0.5% | 1.6% |
| Tax                   |     0.4% | 0.4% |    0.5% |    0.3% | 0.3% |
| **Return on Assets**  | **1.3%** |**1.1%**| **1.7%**| **1.3%**|**1.0%**|

Compared with 9M FY25, the same source shows: Industry NIM 3.0% →
3.2% (i.e. -20 bps YoY), PSU 2.5 vs. 2.7, Pvt-New 3.6 vs. 3.9,
Pvt-Old 3.3 vs. 3.5, SFB 6.1 vs. 7.2. SFBs took the largest NIM hit
(~110 bps) and the largest ROA drop (1.0% vs. 1.5%).

Reading: the headline "industry NIM 3.0%" hides a wide spread.
**SFBs at 6.1% NIM but 1.6% credit costs net to the lowest ROA**
(1.0%); **PSU banks at 2.5% NIM but only 0.3% credit costs deliver
1.1% ROA**; **Private-New at 3.6% NIM with 0.7% credit costs lead at
1.7% ROA**. Bank category drives the ROA outcome more than headline
NIM does.

## 9. The rate-cycle setup, 9M FY26

Source: parsed pages 4–5 of the 9M FY26 deck.

- RBI cut repo by **125 bps since Feb 2025** after a prolonged pause;
  stance moved to "neutral" in June 2025.
- 100 bps CRR reduction in 2025 added ~₹2.5 lakh crore liquidity;
  large OMOs (~₹2 lakh crore) and USD/INR buy-sell swaps (~USD 10 bn)
  on top.
- Pass-through has been uneven: lending rates repriced fast on
  benchmark-linked loans; deposit rates stayed sticky in Q3 FY26.
- "Net effect: Asset yields decline faster than funding costs" — the
  structural reason behind the NIM compression in (2).
- BCG's view: "Lower CD-ratio banks (notably PSBs) are better
  positioned to benefit from the rate-cut cycle with relatively lower
  margin pressure." Consistent with the ROA-tree above where PSU
  margin-trees are flattest.

## What this enables for the project

For `PITCH.md`'s CorpFin v2 / Finance Agent v2 benchmark, this corpus
is a coherent six-period longitudinal view of one well-defined sector.
Wiki-grounded synthesis can answer questions like "how did MSME growth
compare to retail across FY25–FY26?" by walking the table above; the
RAG baseline has to retrieve and re-synthesize each time. That's the
exact contrast the pitch is built to measure.
